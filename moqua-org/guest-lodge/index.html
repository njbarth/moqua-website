About the Guest Lodge

Owasippe is home. For generations of Chicago-area Scouts, the reservation in Michigan has been the place where they first paddled a canoe, hiked a trail, and earned a merit badge under the open sky. The Guest Lodge project exists to keep that connection alive for the next generation.

The Owasippe Staff Association and the Chicago Area Council Board of Directors are working to restore and complete a premier lodging facility at Owasippe Scout Reservation. A dedication ceremony was held on Saturday, May 24, 2014 at LC 2 — the Guest Lodge — followed by a brief open house.

Your support helps ensure that Scouts — and the adult volunteers who serve them — have a welcoming, quality home base at camp. In addition to volunteer time, financial support is critical to completing the project and moving on to the next phase of improvements at the reservation.

Yes, you can help Chicago Area Council fulfill its mission to the youth of Chicago and get them to Owasippe. Become a supporter through Friends of Scouting, or help us complete this project with a direct donation using the Guest Lodge Donation Form.
