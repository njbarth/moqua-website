The Moqua Foundation

Giving Back

The Moqua Foundation was established to commemorate the Vigil Honor members of Owasippe Lodge #7, to preserve their biographies for posterity, and to celebrate their contributions to Scouting and to the youth of Chicago.

The Foundation sponsors the annual Vigil Alumni Dinner, held each November in Chicago, at which one or more Vigil Honor members are recognized and their life stories shared with family, fellow Arrowmen, and Scouting friends. The dinner is an opportunity for Vigil alumni to reconnect, to celebrate their shared commitment to the Scout program, and to honor those who answered the call to service at the highest level.

The Foundation also supports the Owasippe Guest Lodge project, which aims to restore and preserve a premier lodging facility at Owasippe Scout Reservation — ensuring that future generations of Scouts continue to have access to the camp that has defined Scouting in Chicago for over a century.

If you have biographical information, photographs, or other materials related to a Vigil Honor member of Owasippe Lodge #7, we welcome your contributions to the archive. Please use the contact form to reach out.

"The creators of this site hope it will serve as a rallying point for Vigil Honor members past and present to use their collective talents to promote a love of the outdoors and a sense of obligation to serve society as a whole."
