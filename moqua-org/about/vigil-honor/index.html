The Vigil Honor

"We are not members of the Order of the Arrow because we are good campers. We are members because we are willing to give of ourselves in service to others."
Order of the Arrow · Vigil Honor Tradition

The Vigil Honor is the highest honor that the Order of the Arrow can bestow upon its members. It recognizes those who, after much careful observation and thought by a small committee, have demonstrated outstanding service, personal character, and leadership to Scouting and the community.

The Vigil Honor has the most stringent requirements of all OA honors and is granted to not more than two percent of the Lodge's active members annually. Membership cannot be won by a person's own actions or ambitions. It must be earned and awarded by fellow Arrowmen.

A central element of the Vigil Honor is the night of silent meditation — the Vigil — during which the honoree meditates on the obligations of the honor and the greater call to service it represents. The Vigil name bestowed upon each honoree, in the language of the Lenni Lenape, reflects the character and qualities for which they were chosen.

Those so honored are expected to provide an example of selfless service worthy of emulation, and to use their positions of leadership to guide others in the traditions of the Order.
