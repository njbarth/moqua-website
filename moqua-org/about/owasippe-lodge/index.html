Owasippe Lodge #7

A Century of Service in Chicago

Owasippe Lodge #7 is the Order of the Arrow lodge of the Chicago Area Council, Boy Scouts of America. Founded in 1921, the lodge is one of the oldest continuously operating OA lodges in the United States — a distinction that reflects the extraordinary commitment of its members to the Scout program and to the youth of Chicago.

The lodge takes its name from Owasippe Scout Reservation, a 4,000-acre property on the shores of Wolf and Magee Lakes near Whitehall, Michigan. The reservation has been the summer home for hundreds of thousands of Chicago-area Scouts since its founding in 1911 — making it the oldest continuously operating Boy Scout camp in the world.

Owasippe Lodge #7 has been at the forefront of Scouting in Chicago — leading in integration, diversity, and service — for more than a century. Its Vigil Honor members represent the best of this tradition: men who gave generously of their time, talent, and treasure to ensure that the next generation of Chicago's youth had access to the life-changing experience of Scouting at Owasippe.

"Owasippe is home. For generations of Chicago-area Scouts, the reservation in Michigan has been the place where they first paddled a canoe, hiked a trail, and earned a merit badge under the open sky."
