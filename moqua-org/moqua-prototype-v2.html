Mission & History

Leadership in Service

"The watchwords of the Vigil Honor — a call to even greater service to others."
Order of the Arrow · Vigil Honor

The Moqua Foundation honors the men of Owasippe Lodge #7 who received the Vigil Honor — Scouting's highest recognition within the Order of the Arrow, awarded to those who have rendered distinguished service to Scouting and demonstrated an exceptional commitment to cheerful service.

Owasippe Lodge #7 has operated in support of the Chicago Area Council, Boy Scouts of America since 1921 — making it one of the oldest continuously operating OA lodges in the country. The lodge is home to Owasippe Scout Reservation, a 4,000-acre property in Michigan that has shaped the lives of hundreds of thousands of Chicago-area Scouts.

The creators of this site hope it will serve as a rallying point for Vigil Honor members past and present to use their collective talents to promote a love of the outdoors and a sense of obligation to serve society as a whole.

Biography
Life & Service

Frank Samuel Accardi was born on October 23, 1927 at home in the upstairs apartment of an Italian import store at 710 W. Grand Avenue in Chicago. His parents Michael and Josephine were immigrants from Italy. Frank is one of seven children and the family soon moved to Frank's childhood home at 3530 W. Hirsch St. and joined Maternity B.V.M. parish.

Frank worked for several iterations of the same trucking company (ABF) starting as a rate analyst, union steward, and ultimately as the office manager. He and Jo Ann welcomed three children into the world — Charles (1949), Rodger (1951), and Victoria (1956). It was during that time Frank was recruited to join the Committee of Cub Scout Pack 3109 sponsored by the Catholic Church. He was recruited the following year to become the Scoutmaster of Troop 109 — a position he held for 16 years from 1960 until 1976.

In 1963 at a Pathfinder District Camporee at Ft. Dearborn, Frank was tapped out by the Cahokia Chapter Ceremonial Team and began his journey in the Order of the Arrow. In 1968 Frank was called by his Brothers in the Arrow to the Vigil Honor for a life of cheerful service to Scouting. He prayerfully meditated on the evening of August 10, 1968 at Owasippe and the following morning became evermore known as "Nenostam Moewagan — To Show Understanding".

When reflecting back on the Order of the Arrow and the Vigil Honor, Frank believes it is an avenue of spirituality for Scouts and Scouters to help them grow closer to God.

Biography
Life & Service

Grant Francis Muraski was born on May 05, 1939 to Joseph F. Muraski and Lillian Muraski at home on the Northwest side of Chicago. He was the fifth of seven children. Grant attended Our Lady of Grace Grammar School and Lane Tech High School and joined Scouting as a young man, quickly developing a lifelong passion for the outdoor program and the values of the Scout Oath and Law.

Grant's service to Owasippe Lodge #7 and the Chicago Area Council spanned decades. He was recognized for his distinguished contributions with the Vigil Honor — Scouting's highest Order of the Arrow recognition — and honored at the 2018 Vigil Alumni Annual Dinner.

Owasippe Guest Lodge

A project to restore and preserve a premier lodging facility at Owasippe Scout Reservation — connecting generations of Scouts to the camp they love.

Project Active
Owasippe Scout Reservation · Michigan
Project Overview
About the Guest Lodge

Owasippe is home. For generations of Chicago-area Scouts, the reservation in Michigan has been the place where they first paddled a canoe, hiked a trail, and earned a merit badge under the open sky.

The Owasippe Staff Association and the Chicago Area Council have been working to restore and complete a premier lodging facility at Owasippe Scout Reservation. Your support helps ensure that Scouts — and the adult volunteers who serve them — have a welcoming, quality home base at camp.
