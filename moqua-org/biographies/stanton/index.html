Biography
Life & Service

Samuel Cecil Stanton was born on June 28, 1856 in Newtonville, MA to Samuel Stanton (b. 10/01/1821) and Ann Louise Blodgett (b. 11/01/1828). At age 7 he met President Abraham Lincoln via an introduction through his cousin, Edwin M. Stanton, then Attorney General of Ohio, and later Secretary of War during the Civil War.

Dr. Stanton entered the University of Illinois in 1872. He eventually received his Bachelor of Science degree in 1879 from the University of Illinois. Dr. Stanton received his Doctor of Medicine degree from Northwestern University in 1892. Later in life he went on to receive a Master of Science degree from the University of Illinois in 1919. After medical school graduation he became the Superintendent of the Post-Graduate Hospital of Chicago as a consulting gynecologist.

Dr. Stanton has the unique distinction of being the oldest member of the site's honored biographies, born in 1856 – and is considered a founding inspiration for the Order of the Arrow's traditions at Owasippe.
