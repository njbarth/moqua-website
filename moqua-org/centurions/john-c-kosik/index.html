Life & Service

John C. Kosik earned his Eagle Scout Award in 1972 while a Scout in Troop 998 on the Northwest Side of Chicago. He was inducted into Ordeal Membership that same year in July at summer camp. An earnest supporter of the Scout camping program, John served on the staff of Camp Robert Crown at Owasippe from 1974-1981. John sealed his membership with the Brotherhood Honor in July 1973.

At the encouragement of Lodge Adviser Sheridan Nunn, John took an active leadership role in Owasippe Lodge, becoming its Chief in 1977. That same year, he led the Lodge's contingent to NOAC at the University of Tennessee in Knoxville. John's determined and methodical leadership saw growth in Lodge membership and a re-focus on the ceremonials of the Order. John's mantra throughout his tenure was, "dream the great dream, and catch the higher spirit". John was the driving force behind the Lodge's $15,000 pledge to the Council's Capital Campaign and personally arranged for O/A Founder E. Urner Goodman to attend the 50th Anniversary Celebration of Owasippe Lodge in 1979. John received the Vigil Honor on 05/26/1979, becoming known as "Lungwamen Mechen Wenigajekink" – To Dream Great Things.

As an adult, John became the Associate Lodge Adviser and served for three years. He was selected to serve as Lodge Adviser from 1983-1986. After a brief hiatus from Scouting to raise a family, John returned to Scouting as a Board Member of Chicago Area Council, charged with managing an investment portfolio in excess of $15M.
