Mission & History

Leadership in Service

"The watchwords of the Vigil Honor — a call to even greater service to others."
Order of the Arrow · Vigil Honor

The Moqua Foundation honors the men of Owasippe Lodge #7 who received the Vigil Honor — Scouting's highest recognition within the Order of the Arrow, awarded to those who have rendered distinguished service to Scouting and demonstrated an exceptional commitment to cheerful service.

Owasippe Lodge #7 has operated in support of the Chicago Area Council, Boy Scouts of America since 1921 — making it one of the oldest continuously operating OA lodges in the country. The lodge is home to Owasippe Scout Reservation, a 4,000-acre property in Michigan that has shaped the lives of hundreds of thousands of Chicago-area Scouts.

Owasippe Guest Lodge

A project to restore and preserve a premier lodging facility at Owasippe Scout Reservation — connecting generations of Scouts to the camp they love.

Project Active
Owasippe Scout Reservation · Michigan
Project Overview
About the Guest Lodge

Owasippe is home. For generations of Chicago-area Scouts, the reservation in Michigan has been the place where they first paddled a canoe, hiked a trail, and earned a merit badge under the open sky. The Guest Lodge project exists to keep that connection alive for the next generation.

The Owasippe Staff Association and the Chicago Area Council have been working to restore and complete a premier lodging facility at Owasippe Scout Reservation. Your support helps ensure that Scouts — and the adult volunteers who serve them — have a welcoming, quality home base at camp.

In addition to volunteer time, financial support is critical to completing the project and moving on to the next phase of improvements at the reservation.
